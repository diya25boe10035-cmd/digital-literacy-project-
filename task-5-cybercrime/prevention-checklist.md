Prevention Checklist for collage students.

1.	Never enter your UPI PIN to receive money. A PIN is only required to send money or check your balance. If someone asks you to scan a QR code or enter a PIN to "claim a prize" or "receive a refund," it is a scam.


2.	Decline unsolicited UPI requests. Scammers often send "Collect Requests" on apps like Google Pay or PhonePe, hoping you'll approve them quickly. Always read the notification carefully to see if money is being deducted or added.
3.	Know that no law enforcement agency (CBI, Police, Customs) will ever "arrest" you over a video call. If you receive a call claiming a parcel with illegal items has been found in your name, hang up immediately. Do not stay on the call or transfer "clearance fees."
4.	Enable Two-Factor Authentication on all accounts. Use app-based authenticators (like Google Authenticator) for your email, WhatsApp, and social media. This prevents hackers from accessing your accounts even if they steal your password.


5.	Verify "Work-from-Home" or "Scholarship" offers. Be skeptical of high-paying jobs offered via WhatsApp/Telegram that ask for an "initial processing fee" or "security deposit." Genuine organizations never ask for money to hire you.
6.	Avoid banking or shopping on campus/cafe Wi-Fi. Public networks are easily intercepted. If you must use them, use a trusted VPN to encrypt your data, or stick to your mobile hotspot for sensitive tasks.
7.	Only download apps from official stores (Play Store/App Store). Never click on shortened links sent via SMS or WhatsApp claiming your electricity will be cut or your bank account is blocked.
8.	Check your 'Tagged' photos and location settings. Avoid posting photos of your ID cards, hall tickets, or hostel room numbers. Ensure your location services are turned off for social media apps to prevent real-time tracking.
